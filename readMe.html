Alan Turing

Alan Turing was born on June 23, 1912, in London, England. Alan showed signs of being highly intelligent at an early age which allowed him to attend The University Of Cambridge, in England. While working at the college, after graduating, he wrote a paper that presented the notion of a universal machine that would be later known as the Turing Machine. This machine was capable of computing anything that is computable. The main concept of the modern computer was built off of this paper.

Alan Turing was also an essential contributor in World War II. He worked at Bletchley Park, a wartime station, where he participated in wartime code-breaking, particularly that of German ciphers.
